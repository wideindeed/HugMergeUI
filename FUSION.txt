HUGMERGEUI — CAN A 0.5B AND A 3B MODEL BE COMBINED AT ALL?
======================================================================

Spun off from VALIDATION.txt, which is entirely about whether the
conflict/drift_magnitude score predicts merge damage between same-shape
fine-tunes of one base model. This is a different question: can two
different-sized models of the same family - not fine-tunes of a shared
checkpoint, but genuinely different architectures in width and depth -
be combined into one model at all? Standard mergekit merge methods
(TIES, linear, dare_ties, ...) are elementwise and require identical
tensor shapes, so they don't apply here by construction. This file
tracks attempts at something that does.

eval/phase_e_cross_size_fusion.py holds the code for round one.


ROUND ONE: NAIVE ZERO-PAD-AND-AVERAGE - CLEANLY KILLED
----------------------------------------------------------------------------

Picked Qwen2.5-0.5B and Qwen2.5-3B: same family, same tokenizer
(vocab_size=151936), otherwise nothing shape-compatible.

  config field         | 0.5B | 3B
  ----------------------|------|------
  hidden_size            | 896  | 2048
  num_hidden_layers       | 24   | 36
  intermediate_size        | 4864 | 11008
  num_attention_heads       | 14   | 16
  num_key_value_heads        | 2    | 2
  vocab_size                  | 151936 | 151936

Every tensor in the 0.5B is a different shape from its 3B counterpart,
including the (tied) embedding table - its second dimension is
hidden_size, so even that doesn't line up.

The naive approach tested: map each of the 0.5B's 24 layers onto one
of the 3B's 36 layers by proportional index (layer i -> round(i *
35/23), so 24 of the 3B's 36 layers get an injection and 12 are left
untouched); zero-pad every 0.5B tensor independently on each axis up
to its mapped 3B tensor's shape (no attempt at semantic alignment -
just pad with zeros and hope); blend the padded tensor 50/50 with the
real 3B tensor at that position, for every touched tensor, non-layer
tensors (embeddings, final norm) included.

Perplexity on the same 5 fixed passages used throughout VALIDATION.txt:

  model                          | perplexity
  --------------------------------|------------
  Qwen2.5-0.5B (solo)              | 10.71
  Qwen2.5-3B (solo)                 | 6.68
  fused (zero-pad + 50/50 blend)     | 54,805.62

The fused model isn't "degraded," it's destroyed - a perplexity in the
tens of thousands over a ~152k-token vocabulary means it's close to
producing uniform random noise. This is not a subtle result requiring
careful interpretation: the naive approach fails completely and
obviously, on the very first real attempt.

Why, in plain terms: zero-padding doesn't encode "this dimension
doesn't exist for the small model," it encodes "this dimension is
exactly zero," which then gets averaged 50/50 into a real, fully
trained value from the 3B model, corrupting it directly. Every touched
tensor in 24 of the 3B's 36 layers got half its information replaced
by a mix of real 0.5B signal (in the first 896 of 2048 dims, roughly)
and hard zeros (in the rest) - there was never a plausible path to
this working, and the perplexity confirms it did not.

Conclusion, stated plainly: naive zero-pad-and-average is not a
viable cross-size fusion method, full stop. This was expected going
in (see VALIDATION.txt's future-work note, candidate 1 of 3) and is
reported here as a clean negative result, not a setback - it rules
out the cheapest option fast, which is the point of testing it first.

Caveats: only one blend weight (0.5) and one layer-mapping scheme
(proportional index) were tried; a much lower blend weight (e.g. 0.05,
barely nudging the 3B) might avoid outright destruction but would also
likely inject too little of the 0.5B to matter, so it's not obviously
worth chasing. Only one pair (Qwen2.5-0.5B + Qwen2.5-3B) was tested.
No further tuning of this approach was attempted after the result came
back this decisively broken, to avoid wasting effort on a method with
no real path forward - see the future-work note in VALIDATION.txt for
the two untried candidates (function-preserving growth, and knowledge
fusion / logit distillation) that don't share this failure mode.

Reproduce: python eval/phase_e_cross_size_fusion.py (not resumable -
single run, no checkpointing, takes one shot at the whole pipeline).
Requires downloading Qwen2.5-3B (~6GB, not needed by any other round)
in addition to the usual environment; an 8GB-VRAM GPU is sufficient
since the fused model is only run in fp16 inference.
